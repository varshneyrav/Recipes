Recipe Steps

30:00

  1. Step 1: Boil the mixed vegetables along with peas and 1 cup water in a pressure cooker for 1 whistle. Keep aside.
  2. Step 2: Heat oil in a pan. Add chopped onions and fry till golden brown. Add ginger garlic paste and sauté. Now, add tomatoes, green chilies, and all the spices like turmeric powder, chili powder, coriander powder, and cumin powder. Fry till the masala is well combined.
  3. Step 3: Add the cooked vegetables with water, MAGGI Masalaae-Magic, salt as per taste and mix well. Mash the vegetables with a spoon or masher, add amchur powder and mix well.
  4. Step 4: Add the remaining water and simmer till the pav bhaji gravy thickens. Remove from the flame and add lemon juice to zest it up.
  5. Step 5: Garnish the gravy with coriander leaves, and your Mumbai Street Style pav bhaji is ready to serve with toasted Pavs. This easy recipe is perfect for weekends or everyday cooking, featuring readily available ingredients for a delicious and healthy dish the whole family will love. Pav Bhaji

Pav Bhaji, originating from the streets of Mumbai, has gained fans worldwide for its wholesome blend of veggies in a tangy tomato gravy, paired with soft Pav. This versatile dish can be enjoyed at any time—breakfast, lunch, dinner, or as a snack. Historically, Pav Bhaji was created in the 1850s to provide textile mill workers with a quick, affordable, and satisfying meal during their short lunch breaks. A local vendor combined leftovers from other dishes into a spicy bhaji, substituting traditional rice or roti with Pav, giving birth to this beloved dish.
